What it maps

Five words, and the whole landscape fits in them.

SpaghettiMapper's vocabulary is deliberately small. Every map is systems joined by streams, walked by flows, justified by needs, gathered into clusters — nothing else to learn, nothing to misfile.

System

A node in the map — yours, someone else's, or the one nobody will claim. Each system owns a catalog of its entities and fields.

kind: internal | external | unknown

Integration stream

Data moving between two systems, with the facts that matter at 2 a.m.: timing, format, direction, and whether it actually exists yet.

timing: scheduled · format: CSV · direction: uni · status: implemented

Flow

An end-to-end journey through the landscape: ordered steps through streams, stage by stage, from the click to the shipped box.

steps: storefront → orders → erp → 3pl

Business need

The reason a stream exists. A stream can serve several. When nobody can name even one — that's not a gap in the tool, that's a finding.

serves: Order Fulfillment

Cluster

A named group of systems — a domain, an estate, an island. Clusters sit under the tangle as quiet colored plates, carry business needs of their own, and filter the map at the coarsest grain.

Payments · 5 systems · 3 needs

Local-first

Your map lives in a folder, not in a tenant.

One binary, no database, nothing phoning home. Every project is a directory of human-readable JSON — open it in your editor, grep it, put it in git, review the diff like any other change to your architecture.

The server knows its architects — humans log in, AI agents present API tokens — so every edit to the map is attributable. When the map says the ERP feed is scheduled, nightly, CSV, you can see who wrote that down.

Every change is saved automatically as a version you can preview and restore — undo reaches all the way back. Each architect edits their own copy of the map and merges into the shared Main when ready.

Delete a project and it moves to .trash/, because a map of your integrations is not a thing you want to lose to a misclick.

~/.spaghettimapper/ acme-commerce/ project.json systems.json streams.json needs.json flows.json clusters.json layout.json display.json auth/users.json tokens.json ← who drew what .trash/ ← quiet deletes

Honest data

absent ≠ false

Field properties are tri-state: a field nobody documented is a gap, not a fact. SpaghettiMapper never fills silence with defaults — so its analysis can tell you what your landscape is, and separately, what nobody has written down yet. The Analysis panel turns that honesty into findings — fields that move with no business need attached are candidates to cut, or to document.

{ "name": "customer_email", "type": "string", "unique": true } { "name": "legacy_ref" } ← undocumented, not false

catalog coverage: 6/14 fields typed — the map tells you where to dig next

unjustified data in motion: 3 fields — they move between systems, and nobody has written down why
